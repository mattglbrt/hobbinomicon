## The Antelope Fight

The antelope started by grazing as usual. We got off to a great start this time—bone darts actually hit for once and we got our first wound early. The fight was going smoothly until a critical wound reaction triggered a lone rampage: all non-death survivors suffered brain damage, knockdown survivors took damage at every hit location, and standing survivors got bashed and knocked back seven spaces.

That rampage caused some serious problems. Two of our survivors took permanent accuracy injuries (contracure)—both getting minus one permanent accuracy. That's brutal, especially on the guitar player who has all the hunt XP.

## Fishing for Crits

Despite having someone who crits on threes and another setup to crit regularly, we had terrible luck fishing for resources through critical wounds. We managed a few—a large flat tooth and a bladder—but nowhere near what we should have gotten with our luck setup.

The trap card caused problems as usual. We had to carefully manage it with the cat eye circlet, sometimes intentionally triggering it when we could absorb the consequences rather than risk hitting it at a bad time.

## Victory with Scars

We beat the antelope, but we walked away with two permanent accuracy injuries. Next time, I'm definitely going to level two since level one felt almost too easy despite the injuries. We need the resources, and the party is strong enough now.
