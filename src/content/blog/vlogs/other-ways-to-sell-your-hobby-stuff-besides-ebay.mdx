## Transcript

Today is about how to pay for your hobby with the hobby. If you remember early on in my first videos, I had a huge stack of books—literally taller than me from the floor. I spent a whole day posting them to eBay back in November.

## My eBay Disaster

I woke up the next morning to a ton of sales, which was great. But while shipping stuff out, I realized eBay somehow glitched and posted some books twice when I only had one copy. I immediately refunded the second buyers and sent messages explaining what happened. Because I canceled about five orders, eBay tanked my top-performing seller status down to basically eBay jail. They're not showing my auctions to people, so I'm trying to sell new-in-box Warhammer stuff pretty cheap and nobody's buying it.

## The eBay Fee Math

Even when eBay works, the fees eat into your profits. Take a Thousand Sons Boarding Patrol—the going rate is about $190 shipped based on sold listings. Run that through the eBay fees calculator: $190 sale price, about $6 shipping cost including box and bubble wrap, below-standard seller fees, plus the promo ad rates they tack on now. The eBay fee comes out to about $41. So that $190 item nets you about $135 actual profit. When you're shipping one item at a time, you need to calculate your time into those profits too.

## Alternative Selling Methods

My goal for the day was to hit $250 cash. Here are the alternatives to eBay:

### Discord and Facebook Groups

Most Discord channels, Facebook groups, and Reddit communities want what's called a "tag"—a photo with your username or name next to all the items you're selling. It shows you actually have the stuff. A lot of places will take your post down without one.

Take a photo of the tag with all the items, then close-ups of individual pieces, and post it. Don't be the seller who says "I maybe want to sell these someday, send me offers." If you're selling, have a price in mind. The onus is on you.

### Discord Etiquette

I had all pictures ready but didn't include them in my first message since it was already long. I didn't want to spam the feed with pictures if nobody's interested. I listed exactly what I had, the condition, and how much I wanted. If someone's interested, they'll hit me up for photos.

### Reddit MiniSwap

Reddit MiniSwap is a great place to buy, sell, and trade. Always use PayPal Goods and Services—it protects you as a buyer from scams since you can get your money back, and it protects you as a seller with insurance. You can buy shipping labels through PayPal without walking into the post office.

There is a PayPal fee, so either eat it or calculate it into your asking price. I combine the shipping and PayPal fee and just say something like "60 bucks shipped in the US."

### Local Game Stores

I called the LGS, but they had a backlog of people selling stuff. They have you drop it off, then call you in a couple days with an offer. That's a trip there, trip home, second trip there, second trip home—all to not get eBay pricing. At that point I'd just throw it on eBay.

### Video Game Trade-Ins

I remembered I had a bunch of old Switch games I'd already beaten with zero replay value. Traded them in at a local game store that does video games. They double trade-in value on certain days. It killed me to take cash instead of store credit, but I needed the cash.

## Results

The Crucible Guard lot sold almost instantly—$150 in the time it took me to walk from the table back to the office. That tells me I probably had it priced too low, but that's okay. We're not running a business, just trying to fund the hobby.

Between PayPal from Facebook Marketplace and cash from the game store trade-ins, I hit $293 for the day. Don't automatically think you have to eBay everything—the game store gave me more cash than I would have made on eBay after fees. Check what your community has available. You'll be surprised.
