## The Painting Process

Going to use Apple Barrel Nutmeg. I don't actually like this brown itself, but I think it's a good midtone. I'm going to mix it right on the board itself.

This is Bronze Flesh Tone—just pick a warm orange. I'm going to dot this on the high areas. And then I have transparent burnt umber ink that I'll put in the low spots and blend it all together.

## The Wet Mud Look

It looks like a mess, but trust me. Big wide brush—this is the biggest brush I got that's easily accessible right now. Keep in mind this is just the first pass. We're going to put multiple layers on here and hit it with a wash, so don't get too worried about it looking like trash right now.

I washed my brush out, got a glove on, going to take a paper towel and just kind of sponge this off.

One thing I do want to point out: this edge right here is where all that medium ballast was that I didn't like. I'm going to keep the paint thick there because it'll harden and thin out a little bit. If I keep it globbed up there, it'll ease that transition more.

## The Final Pass

One final pass of paper towel. I'm just hitting the whole board going in a circle, making it smaller each time just to make sure I get anything that's pulled up.

Right now because the paint's wet, it just looks like mud—which is pretty much what we want. When it dries, it'll look like earth. All this color variation is from our actual paint. It looks like a big pile of poop right now, but that's good. We want it to look like wet mud.
